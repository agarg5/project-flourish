# 07 — Research Notes (Real-World Grounding)

The game's credibility — and therefore its power to inspire — comes from being
grounded in real science. This file collects the concepts the design draws on,
so content (buildings, tech, species, actions) can be built to *mean something
real*. Use it as a content reference and to keep flavor text accurate.

---

## A. The Core Thesis: Humans as a Keystone Species

From the source article: sustainability is usually framed as *harm reduction*
(shrink your footprint). The more inspiring reframing is **synergy** — humans
having a footprint that is *good* for the environment, the way keystone species
do.

**Keystone species** play a disproportionate role in their ecosystem:
- **Elephants:** carve trails other animals use, migrate long distances
  dispersing seeds, turn over soil and dig dry riverbeds so other species reach
  water and nutrients. Even "destructive" acts (knocking trees) happen at a
  scale that *creates* ecosystem foundations.
- **Beavers:** engineer wetlands that support whole communities.

**Ecological niches** = the "job" each species does for the ecosystem
(pollination, seed dispersal, soil turnover, predation balance). A healthy
ecosystem has its niches filled. → This is why the game's biodiversity score
weights **niche coverage** and **keystone health**, not raw species count.

**Game expression:** keystone species as biome-wide boosters; humans can become
keystone-capable in later ages.

## B. Restoration Ecology (What Humans Can Actively Do)

Real interventions that *improve* ecosystems — these become stewardship actions
and tech in the game:
- Converting **monoculture croplands to mixed/polyculture agriculture**.
- **Combating desertification** (greening degraded land).
- **Rewetting peatlands** to capture carbon.
- **Enhanced rock weathering** for carbon capture.
- Sinking retired structures (e.g. old subway cars) to seed **artificial reefs**
  for marine life.
- **Rewilding** and habitat reconnection.

---

## C. Wildlife-Inclusive & Biophilic Urban Design

Cities can be designed so humans and wildlife *both* flourish — the basis for
the Synergy Age content:
- **Wildlife-inclusive urban design:** integrate animal needs into planning.
- **Wildlife corridors:** connect fragmented habitats across urban areas.
- **The 15-minute city:** walkable access to needs; pairs well with bringing
  nature back into the city (and connects to the well-being model's "amenities"
  and "environmental quality" terms).
- **Biophilic design / green roofs / vertical gardens / "bee hotels":** nature
  woven into the built environment; proximity to nature raises human well-being
  (the biophilia coupling in file 03's Well-being formula).
- **Living buildings:** research into mycelium/mushroom-based construction — the
  article's "buildings made from mushrooms that are themselves living."
- **Land sparing vs. land sharing:** two real strategies for biodiversity-
  friendly development (compact dev preserving large greenspace vs. low-density
  nature-integrated dev) — good material for the sustainable-flavor tech branches.

**Nature-positive framing:** the international goal of halting and reversing
biodiversity loss, toward a vision of humanity *living in harmony with nature*
on a healthy planet — exactly the game's tone.

## D. Beyond Earth: Stewardship at Planetary Scale

The article pushes past coexistence to humans providing ecosystem services no
other species can — the Stewardship Age:
- **Asteroid/planetary defense:** protecting *all* Earth's life from impacts
  (research into this is real). Humanity as the planet's shield.
- **Interstellar pollination:** as bees pollinate while making honey, humans
  could move life/resources between worlds via space travel, helping ecosystems
  take hold elsewhere. The game's horizon goal.

---

## E. The Hestia North Star (Idealized End-State)

From Kurzgesagt's "Why Earth Sucks Compared to the Planet Hestia" (May 2026) and
the **superhabitable world** concept (Heller & Armstrong, 2014): Earth is, by
cosmic standards, a *middling* host for life. Over a quarter of its surface is
desert; poles are locked in ice; oceans average ~3,700 m deep but sunlight only
reaches the top ~200 m — vast **dead zones** where little lives.

A **superhabitable** world fixes these. The "Hestia" thought experiment stacks
real exoplanet/biology science:
- Orbits a stable, long-lived **orange dwarf** star.
- ~**1.3× Earth's radius** → ~70% more surface area (more room for life).
- Strong magnetic field; active plate tectonics stabilizing climate/minerals.
- Surface is an **archipelago** of fragmented continents + many islands.
- Surrounded by **shallow, warm, sunlit seas** — the most productive ecosystems
  on any planet — with almost no barren deep ocean.
- **Denser, oxygen-rich, warm, humid atmosphere**; mild seasons.
- Payoff: could host **hundreds of millions of complex species — ~100× Earth's
  biodiversity**.

**Key design takeaway:** the biggest lever for biodiversity is **eliminating
dead zones** and **maximizing productive (shallow, sunlit, vegetated) habitat
area.** This is exactly what the late-game terraforming does — greening deserts,
creating shallow productive seas, fragmenting habitat to drive diversity —
raising the world's carrying capacity toward a Hestia-like ceiling. The final
emotional beat: *humanity made its world more alive than it found it.*

> Note: Hestia's higher gravity would actually be hard on humans — so in the
> game, treat Hestia as the *biodiversity ideal* to aim the planet toward, not a
> literal human habitat target. The point is a world *teeming* with life.

## F. Content-Building Checklist (When Authoring Data)

For each new building / tech / action / species, ask:
1. Is it grounded in something real from the notes above? (Prefer real names.)
2. Does it reinforce **synergy** (helps both well-being and biodiversity, or
   makes that synergy possible)?
3. Does it keep the tone **hopeful** (good vs. even-better, never dirty/guilt)?
4. Does it keep the player **embedded in** the ecosystem (shape habitat, life
   responds) rather than god-handed?
5. Does flourishing-via-this feel **legible** to the player?

If yes to all, it fits the vision.
